- Project 1: Website -

Reflection

In the process of creating this website, I faced several challenges but was able to overcome most of them through self-improvement and practice. When planning my design, I looked at other portfolio websites for inspiration and found some styles that I liked. However, when I began coding, I realised that many of these designs were too complex and difficult to recreate. To solve this, I chose a simpler design, which gave me more flexibility to focus on other elements such as colour and font. Key decisions I had to make was the font and colours for the website as they are the defining point. To find a suitable font, I used inspiration from previous codes I made and other websites to choose an appropriate one. Eventually, I selected “Luckiest Guy” from Google Fonts for the header and “Roboto Condensed” for body text to suit the title. For colours, I felt that a blue tone would suit my theme nicely so I experimented with light and dark shades to match my background. After changing multiple times, I chose a lighter blue-green, #d1dfea, with a lighter background to match. During the process, I learnt about typography, especially the different kinds of fonts and specific distinctions between the four main font classifications. Additionally, I learnt about the very important responsive design using flexbox to allow layouts to adapt smoothly across different screen sizes and devices. Lastly, learning about Git and Github was very crucial to be able to track changes, collaborate with others and manage code effectively. Despite the finished website looking adequate, it still didn’t meet my expectations as there were multiple challenges I couldn’t overcome. For example, I couldn’t understand why the footer wouldn’t go to the very bottom of the page without “justify-content: space-between;”. For the future, I also hope to add other animation effects like the “hover” to add interactivity for users to make it more interesting.
