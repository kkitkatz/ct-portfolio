- Project 1: Website -

Reflection

In the making of this website, I faced multiple challenges, and overcame most of them through self-growth and improvement. When designing what I wanted my website to look like, I looked through previous portfolio designs from others and found that I liked the style of some. However, after I soon tried to code, I found that the designs were quite complex and difficult to replicate. To overcome this, I opted for a simpler design which allowed me to have more freedom in choosing other aspects like colour and font.

Key decisions I had to make was the font and colours for the website as they are the defining point. To find a suitable font, I used inspiration from previous codes I made and other websites to choose an appropriate one. Eventually, I decided to choose “Luckiest Guy” from Google Fonts for the header, “Ranchers” for second headings and “Roboto Condensed” for body text to suit the title. For colours, I felt that a blue tone would suit my theme nicely so I experimented with light and dark shades to match my background. After changing multiple times, I chose a lighter blue-green, #d1dfea, with a lighter background to match.

During the process, I learnt about typography, especially the different kinds of fonts and specific distinctions between the four main font classifications. Additionally, I learnt about the very important responsive design using flexbox to allow layouts to adapt smoothly across different screen sizes and devices. Lastly, learning about Git and Github was very crucial to be able to track changes, collaborate with others and manage code effectively.

Despite the finished website looking adequate, it still didn’t meet my expectations as there were multiple challenges I couldn’t overcome. For example, I couldn’t understand why the footer wouldn’t go to the very bottom of the page without “justify-content: space-between;”. For the future, I also hope to add other animation effects like the “hover” to add interactivity for users to make it more interesting.
